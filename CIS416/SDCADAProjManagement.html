Why Most SCADA Projects Fail

Research from DNV and TESCO suggests that technical failure is rarely the root cause. Instead, it is a failure of integration.

Failure Root Cause Impact on the Project
Scope Creep (Functional) Adding "nice to have" tags mid-project causes database bloat and PLC latency.
IT/OT Cultural Gap IT security patches crashing legacy HMI drivers (Windows 7/10 compatibility).
Inadequate Baseline Failing to audit existing Modbus registers before upgrading the SCADA host.
Nuisance Alarm Neglect Operators ignoring 1,000+ daily alarms, leading to a missed critical event.

KSA Framework for SCADA Engineers

To lead a capital water/wastewater project, an engineer must balance three distinct pillars:

Category Focus Areas
Knowledge IEC 62443 standards, Modbus function code categories (01-16), Virtualization/Cloud architecture.
Skills HLRA (High Level Risk Assessment), budget justification, drafting Service Level Agreements (SLAs).
Abilities Bridging the gap between IT and maintenance crews; identifying "fumbling" vs. "malicious" actors.
