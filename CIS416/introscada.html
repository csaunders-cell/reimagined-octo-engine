Introduction to Industrial Networks

Week 1: Understanding Industrial Control Systems

Learning Objectives

  • Describe functions of RTU, PLC, DCS, and SCADA.
  • Explain the role of telemetry and communication buses.
  • Understand the Purdue Model (ISA 95).
  • Recognize industrial cybersecurity requirements.

Industrial Processing

ICS systems are designed to control variables in the physical world.

Sectors include:

  • Energy, Water, and Wastewater
  • Critical Manufacturing
  • Chemical and Defense Industrial Base

ICS vs. IT Systems

ICS prioritizes Availability, while IT prioritizes Confidentiality.

Key ICS Requirements

  • Availability: Continuous 24/7 operations.
  • Timeliness: Real-time response constraints.
  • Hardening: Built for harsh industrial environments.

Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs)

Common field devices consisting of CPUs, Power Supplies, and I/O modules.

Remote Telemetry Units (RTUs)

Intelligent control units used for geographically distributed systems like oil & gas pipelines.

Distributed Control Systems (DCS)

Distributes control functions across multiple controllers for complex processes.

SCADA Systems

Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition for monitoring dispersed assets.

Network Architecture Layers

Includes Field Bus Loops, Supervisory Loops, and Enterprise Networks.

Industrial Communication Protocols

From legacy Serial (RS-232/485) to modern Ethernet TCP/IP.

The Purdue Model (ISA 95)

Five levels: Level 0 (Process) to Level 4 (Business Systems).

Safety Systems

SIS (Safety Instrumented Systems) and ESD (Emergency Shutdown Systems) protect life and assets.

Safety Integrity Levels (SIL)

Ratings from SIL 1 (Low) to SIL 4 (Extremely high/Costly).

Summary

Availability and timeliness are key. ISA 95 is the standard architecture framework.
