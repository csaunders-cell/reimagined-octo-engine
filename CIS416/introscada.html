Introduction to Industrial Networks

Week 1: Understanding Industrial Control Systems

Industrial Architecture: Exploring the foundation of critical infrastructure control systems.

Learning Objectives

  • Describe functions of RTU, PLC, DCS, and SCADA.
  • Explain role of metering, telemetry, and communication buses.
  • Understand the Purdue Model (ISA 95).
  • Identify industrial safety and protection systems.
  • Recognize unique ICS cybersecurity requirements.

What is Industrial Processing?

ICS systems control physical variables; IT systems manage data.

Critical Sectors:

Chemical, Energy, Water, Manufacturing, Defense, Healthcare, etc.

ICS vs. IT Systems

ICS: Priority on Availability & Data Integrity.

IT: Priority on Confidentiality.

Key Requirements for Industrial Controllers

  • Availability: Continuous 24/7/365 operations.
  • Timeliness: Real-time response constraints.
  • Interfacing: Industrial I/O ports.
  • Physical Hardening: Harsh environment capable.

Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs)

The most common industrial field device.

  • PSU, CPU, Input Modules, Output Modules, Communication Modules.

Remote Telemetry Units (RTUs)

Small intelligent units for gathering data in remote, geographically distributed locations.

Distributed Control Systems (DCS)

Distributes control across multiple controllers for complex, large-scale processes.

SCADA Systems

Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition for monitoring and managing dispersed assets.

Network Architecture Layers

1. Field Bus Loop, 2. Supervisory Loop, 3. LAN, 4. Enterprise Network.

Industrial Communication Protocols

Transitioning from Serial (RS-232/485) to Ethernet TCP/IP and Wireless.

The Purdue Model (ISA 95)

Five-level integration: Level 0 (Process) to Level 4 (Business Systems).

Safety Systems

SIS (Safety Instrumented Systems), ESD (Emergency Shutdown), Fire/Gas, and Leak Detection.

Safety Integrity Levels (SIL)

Ratings from SIL 1 (Low risk) to SIL 4 (Extremely high complexity/risk).
