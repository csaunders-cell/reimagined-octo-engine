Scenario: A landslide has cut off the only access road to your rural facility. Deliveries are paused for 72+ hours.

Step 1: Establishing Digital Visibility

In a rural hospital, "out of sight" means "out of stock." IT must move from reactive to proactive data:

  • Inventory-to-EHR Sync: IT creates a live data bridge so when a nurse pulls the last sterile kit, a "Supply Alert" appears on the doctor's ordering screen.
  • Low-Bandwidth Dashboards: IT deploys a simplified, text-based inventory dashboard for logistics teams to use over backup satellite links.
Proactive Strategy: IT configures "Predictive Burn Rates" to estimate exactly when oxygen and bandages will hit zero based on patient volume.

Step 2: Virtualizing Limited Resources

Physical supplies are stuck, so IT must "digitize" the staff and the beds:

  • Tele-Health Deployment: IT prioritizes network traffic for video consults so local GPs can speak with urban trauma surgeons.
  • Remote Monitoring: IT sets up "Hospital at Home" kits for stable patients, saving on-site IV fluids for the critically ill.

Step 3: Ensuring Tech Survival

If the landslide cut the internet fiber, IT must keep the clinical systems alive locally:

  • Local Cache Activation: IT switches the EHR to "Local Mode" so patient records are accessible even without a global internet connection.
  • IoT Equipment Health: IT uses sensors to monitor generator fuel levels and vaccine fridge temperatures, preventing secondary spoilage.
