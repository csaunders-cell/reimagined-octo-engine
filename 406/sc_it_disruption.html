This activity explores the immediate demands placed upon the **IT Department** at GlobalTech Electronics following the Force Majeure geopolitical event that halted the 'NovaChip' supply chain.

Step 1: Core Systems & Data Integrity ðŸ“Š

The sudden shutdown of the Veridia fabrication plant and the closure of the Strait of Kaelen trigger several system alarms:

  • The **ERP (SAP/Oracle)** system attempts to process inbound material shipments from Aethel that will never arrive, leading to inventory discrepancies.
  • The **SCM (Supply Chain Management)** software's automated reordering logic for Rare Earths in Aethel enters an infinite loop, continuously attempting and failing to place orders.
  • The **WMS (Warehouse Management System)** at the Terra assembly plant shows a projected material run-out in 48 hours, causing a flood of urgent, manual inquiries from Production Managers.

**IT Challenge:** How does IT prevent cascading system errors and provide accurate, real-time status updates to the executive team?

Step 2: Network & Geopolitical Risks ðŸ”’

The geopolitical conflict has a direct physical and digital impact:

  1. **Connectivity Loss:** The primary leased line (MPLS) providing dedicated, secure network connectivity to the **Veridia Fabrication Plant** is **physically severed** due to the conflict. IT must instantly reroute all necessary functions (e.g., remote server access, data backups) via expensive satellite links or cloud-based recovery zones.
  2. **Data Sovereignty Risk:** Due to government actions in Aethel and Veridia, GlobalTech's IT team must immediately verify that no sensitive corporate or customer data resides on servers or cloud instances physically located within the conflict zones. They initiate an emergency **Data Migration** to a safer geographical region (e.g., Europe or North America).
  3. **Cybersecurity Alert:** State-sponsored hacking groups often exploit global crises. IT security must implement **zero-trust protocols**, enhance firewall monitoring, and scrutinize all inbound network traffic for phishing attempts related to "emergency shipping updates" or "supplier change notices."

Step 3: The IT Service Desk & Business Function Awareness ðŸ“ž

The business crisis turns into an **IT support crisis** as hundreds of employees demand information and workarounds:

  • **Sales Team:** Calls reporting the inability to promise new delivery dates, demanding IT instantly update the **CRM (Customer Relationship Management)** system with accurate inventory projections (which are currently impossible).
  • **Procurement Team:** Needs urgent access to new **supplier databases** and tools for sourcing alternative materials outside of Aethel. They need the IT infrastructure to support a sudden, massive increase in due diligence and vendor screening.
  • **Logistics Team:** Requires IT to immediately deploy a new **Tracking & Visibility platform** to monitor alternative, multi-modal (air/sea/rail) shipping routes, bypassing the closed Strait of Kaelen.

The ability of GlobalTech to adapt depends on IT's ability to **rapidly understand the business impact** and provide new solutions, not just fix existing problems.
