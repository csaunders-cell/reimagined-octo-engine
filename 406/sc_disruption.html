Supply Chain Setup:

Step 2: The Force Majeure Disruption üö®

Event: **Geopolitical Conflict**

A sudden, **unforeseen political crisis** escalates rapidly between the nations of Aethel and Veridia, leading to **military skirmishes** near the shared border and, critically, near the **Strait of Kaelen**.

Disruption Impact:

  • The **Strait of Kaelen** is declared a **No-Go Zone** for international commercial shipping. Insurance companies immediately revoke coverage.
  • The Aethel government imposes an **embargo** on all **Rare Earth** exports.
  • Veridia's **Semiconductor Fabrication** plant is forced to shut down temporarily due to government-mandated safety evacuations.

This event is classified as **Force Majeure**‚Äîan unforeseeable circumstance preventing a party from fulfilling a contract.

Step 3: Immediate Consequences for GlobalTech üõë

GlobalTech's supply chain is frozen. Consider the immediate and downstream effects:

  1. **Input Shortage:** No Rare Earths (from Aethel) can reach the Fabrication Plant (in Veridia) or the Assembly Plant (in Terra).
  2. **Manufacturing Halt:** The Fabrication Plant is offline. Even if it could restart, it has no raw materials.
  3. **Logistics Failure:** The primary shipping route (Strait of Kaelen) is closed, meaning existing inventory is stuck, and alternative, longer, and more expensive air/sea routes must be sought.
  4. **Contractual Issues:** GlobalTech's contracts with suppliers and customers likely contain **Force Majeure clauses**. They must now invoke these clauses, halting production and delaying customer orders without immediate penalty, but still damaging long-term relationships.
