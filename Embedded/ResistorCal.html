Why do we need a resistor?

An LED is a "non-ohmic" device. If you give it too much voltage, it will try to pull infinite current until it pops. A resistor acts as a current limiter.

On our Pico W, the GPIO pins provide 3.3V. Since a Red LED only needs 2.0V, the resistor must "eat" the remaining 1.3V.
